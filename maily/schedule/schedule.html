Weekend Schedule

Three Days of Learning, Creating, and Healing

Photo by Doug Van Sant / MoKi Media

Friday

Kevin Breel

2-3PM

Kick off the weekend listening to Kevin Breel, a mental health advocate and comedian who made his first splash at age 19 with his TED talk “Confessions of a Depressed Comic.” He speaks honestly and openly about his experience living with depression, working to break the stigma. He has been called one of the “most influential millennials,” combining humor with the realities of mental health.

Arts and Crafts

3-6PM

Using your creative side of the brain is healing for your mental health! Get crafty and create art that you can have forever. We will be setting up various stations where you can make something! There will be painting, mosaic making, body painting, drawing, collaging, spraypainting, and so much more. Be creative in a way you never have before!

Silent Disco

6-9PM

Put some headphones on and dance with others in some silent disco action! Move your body and shake off the stigma.

Saturday

Sangu Delle

2-3PM

Hear from Sangu Delle, a Pan-African entrepreneur who knows there is no shame in taking care of your mental health. Being an African male, he was taught to suppress his emotions and “be a man.” He gets personal and shares his experience of dealing with anxiety in a world that is uncomfortable with men expressing their emotions.

Movement

3-5PM

Struggling with mental health can hinder you from moving your body but movement is essential for your mental wellbeing. Discover new ways to move and learn how to incorporate it into your daily life! A range of classes and activities will help you feel good in your body and mind. Dancing, yoga, soft pilates, rollerskating, and so much more!

Miguel, Halsey, and Big Sean

5-10PM

Listen to the music of big pop artists who have their own mental health struggles and speak up about it! They will bring the night to life with their music and speak towards their own experiences.

Sunday

Maggie Newcomb

2-3PM

Comedian and mental health advocate, Maggie Newcomb shares her inspirational story and her battle with Bipolar 1 Disorder. She spreads the word that people should “be proud of who they are no matter what they have been through.” With a sense of humor she hopes to lighten the mood around conversations about mental health and break the stigma and fear that surrounds it.

Discover Support and Resources

3-6PM

End the festival by exploring booths that offer a range of resources and information to support you in your mental health journey. Our partner, the Mental Health Coalition will be offering a plethora of information and destigmatization guidance, don’t forget to check them out! There will be discounted drinks and food, raffle prizes, and goodies. Mingle with loved ones and new friends, connect with organizations, enjoy delicious snacks and drinks, and discover supportive resources!
